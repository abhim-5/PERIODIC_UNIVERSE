Europium (Eu)

Atomic Number 63 | A Rare Earth Element with Unique Properties

Overview

Europium is a chemical element with the symbol Eu and atomic number 63. It is a soft, silvery metal that belongs to the lanthanide series of rare earth elements. Europium is highly reactive and oxidizes quickly in air and water.

Discovered in 1901 by Eugène-Anatole Demarçay, europium is named after the continent of Europe. Its unique fluorescence makes it a key component in various modern applications.

Properties & Uses

  • Atomic Number: 63
  • Atomic Mass: 151.96 u
  • Electron Configuration: [Xe] 4f7 6s2
  • Group / Block: Lanthanides (f-block)
  • Standard State: Solid at 25 °C
  • Melting Point: 822 °C
  • Boiling Point: 1529 °C
  • Density: 5.24 g/cm³

Europium is widely used in phosphorescent materials, particularly in television and computer screens, where it enhances red and blue colors. It is also used in fluorescent lamps, control rods for nuclear reactors, and as a doping agent in certain types of glass.

Historical Perspective

Europium was discovered in 1901 by Eugène-Anatole Demarçay while analyzing rare earth oxides. Its name pays homage to the continent of Europe. The element's fluorescent properties have made it indispensable in lighting and display technologies.

Despite its reactivity, europium continues to be an essential material in modern science and technology.
