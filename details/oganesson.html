Oganesson (Og)

Atomic Number 118 | Synthetic and Highly Radioactive Element

Overview

Oganesson is a chemical element with the symbol Og and atomic number 118. It is a synthetic element that is not found naturally and is produced in particle accelerators. Oganesson is highly unstable, with its most stable isotope, Og-294, having a half-life of about 0.89 milliseconds.

Oganesson was first synthesized in 2002 by a joint team of Russian and American scientists at the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research (JINR) in Dubna, Russia. The element is named in honor of Yuri Oganessian, a pioneer in the field of superheavy element research.

Properties & Uses

  • Atomic Number: 118

Due to its short half-life and instability, oganesson has no practical applications. It is used exclusively in scientific research to study the properties of superheavy elements and to expand our understanding of the periodic table.

Historical Perspective

Oganesson was first synthesized in 2002 by a team led by Yuri Oganessian. Its name honors his pioneering contributions to the discovery of superheavy elements.

Although it has no practical applications, the discovery of oganesson contributes to our understanding of the behavior and stability of elements at the limits of the periodic table.
