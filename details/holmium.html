Holmium (Ho)

Atomic Number 67 | A Rare Earth Element with Unique Magnetic Properties

Overview

Holmium is a chemical element with the symbol Ho and atomic number 67. It is a soft, malleable, and ductile metal belonging to the lanthanide series. Holmium is notable for its exceptionally high magnetic strength among all elements.

Discovered in 1878 by Swiss chemists Marc Delafontaine and Jacques-Louis Soret, holmium was named after Stockholm, the capital of Sweden. Its magnetic properties make it invaluable in various advanced technologies.

Properties & Uses

  • Atomic Number: 67
  • Atomic Mass: 164.93 u
  • Electron Configuration: [Xe] 4f11 6s2
  • Group / Block: Lanthanides (f-block)
  • Standard State: Solid at 25 °C
  • Melting Point: 1474 °C
  • Boiling Point: 2700 °C
  • Density: 8.80 g/cm³

Holmium is primarily used in high-strength magnets and as a neutron absorber in nuclear reactors. It is also used in solid-state lasers and as a dopant in optical materials.

Historical Perspective

Holmium was discovered in 1878 by Delafontaine and Soret while analyzing rare earth oxides. It was later independently confirmed by Swedish chemist Per Teodor Cleve. Its name honors Stockholm, Sweden's capital.

Today, holmium's unique magnetic properties and its role in neutron absorption make it a critical element in advanced scientific and industrial applications.
