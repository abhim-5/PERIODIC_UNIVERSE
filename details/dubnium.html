Dubnium (Db)

Atomic Number 105 | Synthetic and Highly Radioactive Element

Overview

Dubnium is a chemical element with the symbol Db and atomic number 105. It is a synthetic element that is not found naturally and is produced in particle accelerators. Dubnium is highly unstable, with its most stable isotope, Db-268, having a half-life of about 32 hours.

Dubnium was first synthesized in 1967 by both Soviet and American scientists, and its name honors the Russian town of Dubna, home to the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research.

Properties & Uses

  • Atomic Number: 105

  • Electron Configuration: [Rn] 5f14 6d3 7s2
  • Group / Block: 5 (d-block)
  • Standard State: Solid (predicted)
  • Melting Point: Unknown
  • Boiling Point: Unknown

Dubnium is primarily used for scientific research into the properties of superheavy elements. Due to its short half-life and rarity, it has no practical applications outside of academic studies.

Historical Perspective

Dubnium was first synthesized in 1967 by a Soviet team at the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research in Dubna, Russia, and independently by an American team at the Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory. The naming of the element was a point of contention between the two groups, but the name "dubnium" was internationally recognized in 1997.

As a synthetic element, dubnium’s discovery helped expand our understanding of the periodic table and the behavior of superheavy elements.
