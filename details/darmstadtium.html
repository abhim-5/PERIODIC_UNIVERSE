Darmstadtium (Ds)

Atomic Number 110 | Synthetic and Highly Radioactive Element

Overview

Darmstadtium is a chemical element with the symbol Ds and atomic number 110. It is a synthetic element that is not found naturally and is produced in particle accelerators. Darmstadtium is highly unstable, with its most stable isotope, Ds-281, having a half-life of about 10 seconds.

Darmstadtium was first synthesized in 1994 by a team of scientists at the GSI Helmholtz Centre for Heavy Ion Research in Darmstadt, Germany. The element is named after the city of Darmstadt, where it was discovered.

Properties & Uses

Due to its short half-life and instability, darmstadtium has no practical applications. It is used exclusively in scientific research to study the properties of superheavy elements and to expand our understanding of the periodic table.

Historical Perspective

Darmstadtium was first synthesized in 1994 by a team of German researchers led by Peter Armbruster and Gottfried Münzenberg. The element was named in honor of the city of Darmstadt, where the discovery was made. Its synthesis marked another milestone in the exploration of superheavy elements.

Although it has no practical applications, darmstadtium’s discovery contributes to the ongoing research into the properties and behaviors of elements at the extremes of the periodic table.
