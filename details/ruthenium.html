Ruthenium (Ru)

Atomic Number 44 | Rare Transition Metal

Overview

Ruthenium is a chemical element with the symbol Ru and atomic number 44. It is a rare transition metal belonging to the platinum group of the periodic table. Known for its hardness and resistance to corrosion, ruthenium is widely used in electronics and as a catalyst.

Discovered in 1844 by Karl Ernst Claus, ruthenium was named after Ruthenia, the Latin name for Russia. Despite its rarity, it plays a significant role in industrial and technological applications.

Properties & Uses

  • Atomic Number: 44
  • Atomic Mass: 101.07 u
  • Electron Configuration: [Kr] 4d7 5s1
  • Group / Block: 8 (d-block)
  • Standard State: Solid at 25 °C
  • Melting Point: 2334 °C
  • Boiling Point: 4150 °C
  • Radioactive: No

Ruthenium is used in electronics for plating electrical contacts and as a catalyst in chemical reactions. It is also employed in alloys to improve hardness and resistance to wear. In recent years, ruthenium has been explored for its potential applications in data storage and solar cells.

Historical Perspective

Ruthenium was discovered in 1844 by Russian chemist Karl Ernst Claus while analyzing platinum ores. Its unique properties were quickly recognized, leading to its use in a variety of applications.

Today, ruthenium continues to play a vital role in advanced technologies, including semiconductors and renewable energy systems.
