Mercury (Hg)

Atomic Number 80 | Unique Liquid Metal

Overview

Mercury is a chemical element with the symbol Hg (from Latin: hydrargyrum) and atomic number 80. It is the only metal that is liquid at standard conditions for temperature and pressure. Known for its silvery appearance, mercury has unique physical and chemical properties.

Mercury has been known since ancient times and was historically used in alchemy and medicine. Today, its applications are limited due to its toxicity, but it remains essential in certain scientific and industrial uses.

Properties & Uses

  • Atomic Number: 80
  • Atomic Mass: 200.59 u
  • Electron Configuration: [Xe] 4f14 5d10 6s2
  • Group / Block: 12 (d-block)
  • Standard State: Liquid at 25 °C
  • Melting Point: −38.83 °C
  • Boiling Point: 356.73 °C
  • Radioactive: No

Mercury is used in thermometers, barometers, and other scientific instruments. It is also employed in the production of chlorine and caustic soda and in mercury-vapor lamps. However, due to its toxicity, safer alternatives are now preferred for many of these applications.

Mercury in Action

Mercury's unique properties make it a fascinating element to study. Its liquid state at room temperature and density allow for various experiments and demonstrations, showcasing the wonders of chemistry and physics.

Historical Perspective

Mercury has been used for thousands of years, dating back to ancient Egypt and China. Its association with alchemy and its use in medicinal compounds gave it a mystical reputation. By the 20th century, its toxic effects were better understood, leading to stricter regulations.

Today, mercury's use is heavily restricted, but it remains crucial in specific scientific and industrial contexts, showcasing its enduring importance despite its risks.
