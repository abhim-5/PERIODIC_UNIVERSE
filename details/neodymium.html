Neodymium (Nd)

Atomic Number 60 | A Rare Earth Element Critical for High-Tech Applications

Overview

Neodymium is a chemical element with the symbol Nd and atomic number 60. It is a soft, silvery metal belonging to the lanthanide series. Neodymium is known for its high magnetic strength and is a key component in powerful permanent magnets.

Discovered in 1885 by Carl Auer von Welsbach, neodymium was separated from praseodymium as part of the element "didymium." Its unique properties make it indispensable in many modern technologies.

Properties & Uses

  • Atomic Number: 60
  • Atomic Mass: 144.242 u
  • Electron Configuration: [Xe] 4f4 6s2
  • Group / Block: Lanthanides (f-block)
  • Standard State: Solid at 25 °C
  • Melting Point: 1024 °C
  • Boiling Point: 3074 °C
  • Density: 7.01 g/cm³

Neodymium is used in powerful permanent magnets, which are essential in electric motors, wind turbines, and headphones. It is also used in glass coloring, as a laser medium, and in the production of specialized alloys.

Historical Perspective

Neodymium was discovered in 1885 by Carl Auer von Welsbach during the separation of "didymium." Its name derives from the Greek words "neos" (new) and "didymos" (twin).

Today, neodymium plays a crucial role in the development of green technologies and advanced electronic devices.
