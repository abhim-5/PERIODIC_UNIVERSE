Ytterbium (Yb)

Atomic Number 70 | A Rare Earth Metal with Unique Physical Properties

Overview

Ytterbium is a chemical element with the symbol Yb and atomic number 70. It is a soft, silvery metal belonging to the lanthanide series of rare earth elements. Ytterbium exhibits unique physical properties, such as variable valence states, making it valuable in specific applications.

Discovered in 1878 by Swiss chemist Jean Charles Galissard de Marignac, ytterbium was named after the Swedish village of Ytterby, where its ore was first identified.

Properties & Uses

  • Atomic Number: 70
  • Atomic Mass: 173.04 u
  • Electron Configuration: [Xe] 4f14 6s2
  • Group / Block: Lanthanides (f-block)
  • Standard State: Solid at 25 °C
  • Melting Point: 824 °C
  • Boiling Point: 1196 °C
  • Density: 6.90 g/cm³

Ytterbium is used in atomic clocks, as it enhances precision through its unique energy levels. It is also employed in metallurgy to improve the strength and grain refinement of certain alloys. Additionally, ytterbium is utilized in fiber optic technology and as a dopant in lasers.

Historical Perspective

Ytterbium was discovered in 1878 by Jean Charles Galissard de Marignac, who identified it in the mineral erbia. Its name pays homage to Ytterby, Sweden, the source of many rare earth discoveries.

Today, ytterbium’s diverse applications, ranging from atomic clocks to advanced materials, highlight its scientific and industrial significance.
