Terbium (Tb)

Atomic Number 65 | A Rare Earth Element with Specialized Uses

Overview

Terbium is a chemical element with the symbol Tb and atomic number 65. It is a silvery-white, malleable metal and one of the lanthanides in the rare earth group. Terbium is known for its magnetic, optical, and electronic properties.

Discovered in 1843 by Swedish chemist Carl Gustaf Mosander, terbium was named after the village of Ytterby in Sweden, where its ore was first identified. It is often used in modern technologies.

Properties & Uses

  • Atomic Number: 65
  • Atomic Mass: 158.925 u
  • Electron Configuration: [Xe] 4f9 6s2
  • Group / Block: Lanthanides (f-block)
  • Standard State: Solid at 25 °C
  • Melting Point: 1356 °C
  • Boiling Point: 3230 °C
  • Density: 8.23 g/cm³

Terbium is widely used in phosphors for fluorescent lamps and LED lighting, as well as in solid-state devices and as a dopant in optical materials. Its alloys are used in magnetic materials and fuel cells.

Historical Perspective

Terbium was first identified in 1843 by Carl Gustaf Mosander during the analysis of rare earth oxides. The element's name originates from Ytterby, Sweden, a location historically significant in rare earth discoveries.

Modern applications of terbium highlight its importance in energy-efficient lighting and advanced magnetic materials, making it a vital component of green technology.
