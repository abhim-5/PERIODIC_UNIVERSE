Flerovium (Fl)

Atomic Number 114 | Synthetic and Highly Radioactive Element

Overview

Flerovium is a chemical element with the symbol Fl and atomic number 114. It is a synthetic element that is not found naturally and is produced in particle accelerators. Flerovium is highly unstable, with its most stable isotope, Fl-289, having a half-life of about 2.6 seconds.

Flerovium was first synthesized in 1998 by a team of Russian scientists at the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research (JINR) in Dubna, Russia. The element is named after the Flerov Laboratory of Nuclear Reactions, which in turn honors physicist Georgy Flyorov.

Properties & Uses

Due to its short half-life and instability, flerovium has no practical applications. It is used exclusively in scientific research to study the properties of superheavy elements and to expand our understanding of the periodic table.

Historical Perspective

Flerovium was first synthesized in 1998 by a team of researchers led by Yuri Oganessian. The element was named after the Flerov Laboratory of Nuclear Reactions, honoring the legacy of nuclear physicist Georgy Flyorov.

Although it has no practical applications, the discovery of flerovium contributes to our understanding of superheavy elements and their behavior in extreme conditions.
