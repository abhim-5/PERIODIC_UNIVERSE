Potassium (K)

Atomic Number 19 | Essential Alkali Metal

Overview

Potassium is a chemical element with the symbol K (from Neo-Latin "kalium") and atomic number 19. It is a soft, silvery-white metal belonging to the alkali metal group. Potassium is essential for life, playing a critical role in nerve function, muscle contraction, and maintaining fluid balance in living organisms.

Discovered in 1807 by Sir Humphry Davy, potassium is the seventh most abundant element in the Earth's crust. It is primarily found in minerals such as sylvite, carnallite, and kainite.

Properties & Uses

  • Atomic Number: 19
  • Atomic Mass: 39.098 u
  • Electron Configuration: [Ar] 4s1
  • Group / Block: 1 (s-block), Alkali Metal
  • Standard State: Solid at 25 °C
  • Melting Point: 63.5 °C
  • Boiling Point: 759 °C
  • Radioactive: No

Potassium is widely used in agriculture as a component of fertilizers, where it helps promote plant growth. It is also utilized in the manufacture of glass, soaps, and detergents. In the laboratory, potassium is used in various chemical reactions and as a reagent for organic synthesis.

Historical Perspective

Potassium was first isolated in 1807 by Sir Humphry Davy through the electrolysis of molten potash. It was the first metal to be isolated using electricity. The name "potassium" is derived from "potash," a term used to describe potassium-containing compounds.

Today, potassium remains vital in agriculture and biology, underscoring its importance to both plant and animal life.
