Silver (Ag)

Atomic Number 47 | Precious Metal

Overview

Silver is a chemical element with the symbol Ag and atomic number 47. It is a soft, white, lustrous transition metal with the highest electrical and thermal conductivity of all metals. Silver is widely used in jewelry, electronics, and as a monetary standard throughout history.

Known since ancient times, silver has been valued for its beauty and rarity. Today, it remains an important material in various industries and technologies.

Properties & Uses

  • Atomic Number: 47
  • Atomic Mass: 107.868 u
  • Electron Configuration: [Kr] 4d10 5s1
  • Group / Block: 11 (d-block)
  • Standard State: Solid at 25 °C
  • Melting Point: 961.78 °C
  • Boiling Point: 2162 °C
  • Radioactive: No

Silver is extensively used in electronics due to its excellent conductivity. It is also widely used in solar panels, water filtration, and antimicrobial applications. Additionally, silver is a popular material for coins, jewelry, and tableware.

Historical Perspective

Silver has been known and used by humans for thousands of years. Its name is derived from the Anglo-Saxon word "seolfor" and the Latin word "argentum." Silver's unique properties and historical value have made it a key material in cultural and technological advancements.

Today, silver continues to play a vital role in industries ranging from renewable energy to healthcare.
