Moscovium (Mc)

Atomic Number 115 | Synthetic and Highly Radioactive Element

Overview

Moscovium is a chemical element with the symbol Mc and atomic number 115. It is a synthetic element that is not found naturally and is produced in particle accelerators. Moscovium is highly unstable, with its most stable isotope, Mc-290, having a half-life of about 0.65 seconds.

Moscovium was first synthesized in 2003 by a joint team of Russian and American scientists at the Joint Institute for Nuclear Research (JINR) in Dubna, Russia. The element is named after Moscow, the capital of Russia.

Properties & Uses

  • Atomic Number: 115

  • Group / Block: 15 (p-block)
  • Standard State: Solid (predicted)
  • Melting Point: Unknown
  • Boiling Point: Unknown

Due to its short half-life and instability, moscovium has no practical applications. It is used exclusively in scientific research to study the properties of superheavy elements and to expand our understanding of the periodic table.

Historical Perspective

Moscovium was first synthesized in 2003 by a team led by Yuri Oganessian. Its name honors Moscow, reflecting the collaborative effort of Russian and American scientists in its discovery.

Although it has no practical applications, the discovery of moscovium provides valuable insights into the properties of superheavy elements and their behavior under extreme conditions.
