Gadolinium (Gd)

Atomic Number 64 | A Versatile Rare Earth Element

Overview

Gadolinium is a chemical element with the symbol Gd and atomic number 64. It is a silvery-white, malleable metal belonging to the lanthanide series of rare earth elements. Gadolinium is known for its magnetic properties and its ability to absorb neutrons.

Discovered in 1880 by Swiss chemist Jean Charles Galissard de Marignac, gadolinium was named after Finnish chemist Johan Gadolin. It has become an essential element in various industrial and scientific applications.

Properties & Uses

  • Atomic Number: 64
  • Atomic Mass: 157.25 u
  • Electron Configuration: [Xe] 4f7 5d1 6s2
  • Group / Block: Lanthanides (f-block)
  • Standard State: Solid at 25 °C
  • Melting Point: 1312 °C
  • Boiling Point: 3273 °C
  • Density: 7.90 g/cm³

Gadolinium is widely used as a contrast agent in magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) due to its paramagnetic properties. It is also used in the manufacturing of high-performance magnets, nuclear reactor control rods, and phosphors in color television and LED screens.

Historical Perspective

Gadolinium was first isolated in 1880 by Jean Charles Galissard de Marignac. Its name honors Johan Gadolin, a pioneer in the study of rare earth elements. The element's unique properties have made it valuable in modern medical imaging and electronic technologies.

Today, gadolinium continues to play a vital role in scientific research and industrial applications, particularly in the fields of healthcare and clean energy.
